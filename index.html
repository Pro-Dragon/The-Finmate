The 50/30/20 Rule

A simple budgeting framework to help you manage your money effectively and build financial stability.

Needs

50%

Essential expenses you can't avoid:

  • Rent or mortgage
  • Groceries and food
  • Utilities and bills
  • Healthcare
  • Transportation

Wants

30%

Non-essential expenses that improve your life:

  • Dining out
  • Entertainment
  • Shopping
  • Vacations
  • Subscriptions

Savings

20%

Building your financial future:

  • Emergency fund
  • Retirement accounts
  • Debt repayment
  • Investments
  • Future goals

Build Your Financial Portfolio

A well-rounded financial portfolio consists of these four essential components. Each plays a crucial role in your financial well-being.

Savings

Your foundation for financial security. Start with a savings account that offers reasonable interest and easy access for emergencies.

Recommended options:
  • High-yield savings accounts
  • Fixed deposits for short-term goals

Investments

Grow your wealth over time. Diversify across different asset classes based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

Recommended options:
  • Mutual funds and ETFs
  • Stocks for long-term growth

Insurance

Protect yourself and your family from unexpected financial burdens. Insurance is not an expense but a safety net.

Essential coverage:
  • Health insurance
  • Term life insurance

Emergency Fund

Your financial cushion for unexpected situations. Aim to save 3-6 months of essential expenses in an easily accessible account.

Key features:
  • Liquid and easily accessible
  • Separate from regular savings

Avoid Financial Traps

Be aware of these common financial pitfalls that can derail your financial progress and lead to long-term problems.

High EMIs

Committing to high monthly EMIs can strain your budget and leave little room for savings or emergencies.

Warning: Keep your total EMIs below 40% of your take-home income.

Instant Loan Apps

These apps often charge exorbitant interest rates and use predatory collection practices.

Warning: Some instant loan apps charge up to 36% annual interest or more.

Credit Card Debt

Revolving credit card debt with high interest rates can quickly spiral out of control.

Warning: Credit cards in India typically charge 36-42% annual interest on unpaid balances.

Online Betting

Gambling and betting apps promise quick money but almost always lead to financial losses.

Warning: The house always wins in the long run. Betting is not a financial strategy.
